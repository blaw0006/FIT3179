The Fourth Wave

In late February, 2021, having already suffered immense casualties from the first three waves, Malaysia began its national immunisation programme in an attempt to reduce the severity of the forecasted waves of COVID-19. This visualisation examines the efficiency and speed with which different Malaysian states administered vaccines to its population over the first month of the programme.

Population size and Vaccination efficiency

Some states were more responsive than others to the news of the national immunisation programme, with Putrajaya reaching over 10% of first dose vaccinations by the end of the first month. Perhaps unsurprisingly, the states with the largest populations were not the most organised - Selangor and Sabah both had very low vaccination rates by the start of April. Most of the states had a low efficiency, since a majority of the population registered for a first dose, but had not received one by the end of the first month.

First and second dose vaccinations over the first month

Taking a closer look at first and second dose vaccinations, it appears that most of the population that had received a first dose also received a second dose by the end of the first month of the programme. Still, both numbers remained relatively low for most states.
